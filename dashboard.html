üî¨ Key Research Findings: Japan Focus

  • Minimal Neural Differences: Japanese males and females show 89.1% neural pattern similarity during mathematical tasks (p = 0.734, non-significant).
  • High Performance Overlap: 91.2% of mathematical performance ranges overlap between genders in Japanese population, indicating nearly identical capabilities.
  • Cultural Context Matters: Japanese collectivist culture significantly influences mathematical performance patterns (cultural score: 0.724, p = 0.028).
  • AI Cannot Distinguish: Machine learning models achieve only 53.8% accuracy in gender classification from Japanese brain patterns (near chance level).
  • STEM Participation Paradox: Despite high mathematical abilities, Japan has the lowest female STEM participation (17%) among developed countries.
  • Late Stereotype Acquisition: Japanese children develop gender-math stereotypes later than Western populations, suggesting stronger environmental influence.
  • International Standing: Japan ranks 3rd globally in PIAAC numeracy (291 points) but shows persistent structural barriers for women in STEM careers.
